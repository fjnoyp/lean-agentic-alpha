A3.1 Macro Base Rates & 12-Month Forecasts (May 2026 – May 2027)

Archer Aviation eVTOL Market Context

Analysis Period: May 2026 – May 2027 (12-month forward outlook)

Executive Summary

This specialist report quantifies six critical exogenous macro variables that gate the three forward drivers identified in A2.3:

  1. US Economic Growth & Demand — affects commercial revenue timing and customer capex appetite
  2. Federal Reserve Policy & Interest Rates — affects capital availability for production ramp and customer financing
  3. Labor Costs & Manufacturing Capacity — affects production feasibility and unit economics
  4. Energy Costs (Oil, Electricity) — affects eVTOL value proposition vs. traditional aviation
  5. Credit Market Conditions — affects dilutive vs. non-dilutive capital raises for Archer
  6. Inflation & Wage Growth — affects operational costs and customer demand elasticity

All forecasts are derived from primary sources: Federal Reserve FOMC projections (March 2026), U.S. Energy Information Administration Short-Term Energy Outlook, U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, and consensus institutional research from JP Morgan, Goldman Sachs, and others.

Key Finding: The 12-month window is characterized by gradually declining interest rates but persistent labor shortages and elevated inflation, creating a mixed environment for capital availability (improving) offset by production cost pressure (worsening) and modest demand growth (2.3% GDP forecast).

1. US GDP Growth & Economic Demand

Current State (May 2026)

The U.S. economy is forecast to expand 2.3–2.5% in 2026, according to the most recent Federal Reserve FOMC projections (March 18, 2026). The median FOMC projection is 2.4% for 2026. Goldman Sachs Research projects slightly stronger 2.5% growth (Q4 YoY). Deloitte forecasts 2.2% for 2026, though it projects a 0.4% contraction in 2027 — a more pessimistic tail.

12-Month Forward (May 2027 Forecast)

The Federal Reserve's March 2026 SEP projects 2.3% real GDP growth for 2027, reflecting modest deceleration from 2026. This growth trajectory is sufficient for corporate capex spending (airlines, militaries) but not accelerating demand for premium transportation.

Relevance to A2.3 Forward Drivers

Commercial Revenue Launch: GDP growth directly affects United Airlines' appetite for premium urban air mobility pilots and corporate travel adoption. At 2.3% growth, airline capex growth will likely remain modest (mid-single digits), constraining immediate willingness to scale eVTOL operations beyond pilot programs. Per A2.3, the revenue inflection requires USAF or United to begin paying for operations in H2 2026–Q1 2027; if GDP growth disappoints below 2%, corporate travel demand could slip, delaying the revenue decision.

Type Certification: Regulatory timelines are not directly affected by GDP, but government budget cycles (USAF procurement) are. Moderate GDP growth supports continued defense spending.

Battery & Capital: GDP growth does not directly affect battery technology roadmaps.

Confidence Level

HIGH — Based on official Federal Reserve FOMC Summary of Economic Projections, released March 18, 2026, with cross-triangulation from Goldman Sachs and Deloitte consensus.

Lower interest rates reduce the cost of capital for: - Archer's debt refinancing or new borrowing - Venture capital returns (lower discount rates improve VC deployment appetite) - Customer financing for airlines/military (capex financing costs for eVTOL fleets)

A 50 basis point decline in Fed funds over 12 months is modestly favorable for capital markets but not transformative. A2.3 estimates that Archer requires $300–500M additional capital by 2027; lower rates reduce the hurdle rate for raising debt (non-dilutive) vs. equity (dilutive).

HIGH — Based on official FOMC dot plot projections from March 2026.

Relevance to A2.3

eVTOL Value Proposition: Higher oil prices increase the cost of traditional helicopter/jet fuel, improving the relative economics of electric aviation. Per A2.3, battery performance (400 Wh/kg) is sufficient for regional eVTOL routes but not yet for long-haul airline interline operations. With Brent crude at $76–88/bbl in 2026–2027, the fuel cost advantage of eVTOL (electricity at ~$0.15/kWh vs. Jet-A at ~$2/gallon) remains compelling for city-pair operations.

Declining oil prices (from $115 in Q2 2026 toward $76 in 2027) would reduce the relative advantage of eVTOL, potentially delaying customer adoption. However, the absolute cost advantage remains: electric powertrains at $0.05–0.08/mile vs. jet fuel at $0.12–0.15/mile for traditional helicopters.

Confidence Level: MEDIUM — Oil price forecasts depend heavily on geopolitical disruptions (Iran Strait of Hormuz conflict) and OPEC production decisions.
