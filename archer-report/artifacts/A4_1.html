A4.1 — Peer Metrics Comparison: Archer Aviation eVTOL Peers

Analysis Period: May 2026 (snapshot), 12-month forward outlook to May 2027

Executive Summary

Archer Aviation (ACHR) operates in a competitive but pre-revenue eVTOL market where valuation multiples diverge sharply based on regulatory progress and near-term revenue timing. Five serious competitors are tracked below: (Joby Aviation, Vertical Aerospace, Beta Technologies, EHang Holdings, and Lilium).

Key Comparative Finding: As of May 2026, Joby Aviation trades at the highest absolute valuation ($10.32B market cap) but on comparable per-aircraft EV/Revenue multiples to Archer ($4.9B market cap). Both derive value from near-term revenue visibility (Joby: Dubai 2026, Archer: Abu Dhabi + eIPP 2026). In contrast, Vertical Aerospace trades at a severe discount ($338.7M market cap) due to delayed certification and cash runway concerns, while Beta Technologies ($3.38B market cap) has declined significantly from IPO ($7.44B valuation) despite strong revenue growth and backlog. EHang Holdings ($748M) is the only profitable, revenue-generating peer, operating autonomously in Asia with near-zero debt. Lilium ($32.89M) has entered liquidation, illustrating execution risk in the sector.

For the 12-month horizon (May 2026–May 2027), the peer set differentiates primarily on: (1) regulatory approval timing (Joby FAA Stage 4 vs. Archer Type Cert late 2026/Q1 2027), (2) first revenue recognition (Joby Dubai Q2-Q3 2026 vs. Archer Abu Dhabi Q3 2026 + eIPP H2 2026), (3) capital efficiency (cash burn and runway extension), and (4) production scale (Joby: 4/month by 2027; Archer: 252/year by 2027; Beta: $4B backlog but FAA cert years away).
