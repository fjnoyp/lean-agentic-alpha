A3.2 Sector Growth — Archer Aviation (eVTOL / Urban Air Mobility)

Analysis Period: May 2026 – May 2027 (12-month horizon); Projections through 2030

Summary

The eVTOL/Urban Air Mobility (UAM) sector is projected to grow 27–36% CAGR through 2030, with total addressable market (TAM) estimates ranging from $4.4B to $23.5B by 2030, depending on segment and geographic scope. Archer's core opportunity is anchored by three use cases: (1) airport shuttles (32.4% CAGR), driven by United Airlines' conditional 200-unit order and infrastructure partnerships; (2) defense/military (~18% of global eVTOL market, $12.7B in 2024, 9.5% CAGR), supported by the $142M USAF contract; and (3) intercity/regional transport, which exhibits highest growth but lower near-term revenue potential. Archer's management projects production ramp to 252 units (2027) and 650 units (2028), implying potential 10–15% global market share by 2030 if competitive positions hold. Over the 12-month window (May 2026–May 2027), the sector's growth inflection depends on regulatory approval milestones, battery technology validation (400+ Wh/kg), and initial commercial service launches.

Sector Growth Metrics & Archer Sensitivity

1. eVTOL Market TAM & CAGR (2026–2030)

Metric: Global eVTOL Aircraft Market CAGR through 2030

The eVTOL aircraft market was valued at USD 1.19 billion in 2025 and is forecasted to reach USD 4.36 billion by 2030 at a 29.65% CAGR. An alternative projection by another source estimates the market to grow from USD 1.35 billion (2023) to USD 28.6 billion (2030), at a CAGR of 54.9% from 2024 to 2030, reflecting upside scenarios where production ramps aggressively and regulatory pathways accelerate. A more conservative estimate from Grand View Research puts the market at USD 4.67 billion by 2030, at 35.3% CAGR from 2024 to 2030.

Data Triangulation: Three independent sources yield a consensus range of 29.6% to 35.3% CAGR for the core aircraft/propulsion segment through 2030, with outliers reaching 54.9% if capacity ramps are realized and regulatory timelines accelerate. The discrepancy reflects uncertainty in production ramp speed (Archer and competitors must first achieve FAA Type Certification and scale manufacturing).

Archer Sensitivity: HIGH. Archer's revenue and profitability scale directly with sector TAM penetration. Per A2.3, Archer's commercial revenue launch is the primary TSR driver (+10% expected impact), and any acceleration in sector-wide TAM growth would strengthen the business case for earlier, larger-scale production ramps and customer orders.

2. Airport Shuttle Segment Growth & United Airlines Partnership TAM

Metric: Passenger-transport segment CAGR (airport shuttle focus)

By application, the passenger transport segment is projected to grow at the highest CAGR of 32.4% from 2026 to 2030, indicating strong demand in air taxi services. The urban air mobility market is estimated to be USD 4.6 billion in 2024 and is projected to reach USD 23.5 billion by 2030, at a CAGR of 31.2% between 2024 and 2030. More specifically, airport shuttle services are identified as the most promising near-term application, with in-vehicle time reduced by approximately 80% relative to ground transport. A case study on deployment infrastructure found approximately 17,434 passengers per day could be transported by a fleet of 930 eVTOLs of varying models, utilizing up to 403 vertiports.

Archer Partnership: United Airlines and Archer have selected O'Hare International Airport (ORD) to Vertiport Chicago as their first commercial eVTOL route, with plans to support safe integration of eVTOL aircraft operations into the City's transportation ecosystem in 2025. Additionally, United Airlines passengers will be able to book flights on Archer's Midnight eVTOL air taxi to travel to and from New York City's airports. United Airlines and its partner Mesa Airlines have provisionally agreed to buy up to 200 of Archer Aviation's four-passenger, all-electric eVTOL aircraft.

Pricing & Revenue Per Trip: eVTOL passenger pricing is projected to range from $5-8 per mile initially (comparable to Uber Black), with prices remaining premium ($75-150 per trip) through 2030. A 30-mile airport-to-downtown trip would cost approximately $150-240.

Archer Sensitivity: VERY HIGH. Per A2.3, Commercial Revenue Launch is Archer's primary TSR inflection point (expected +10% impact, +18% upside). The United partnership targets the highest-growth segment (32.4% CAGR), and early commercial success in Chicago and NYC would validate the aircraft and unlock scaling through additional airport hubs. If United exercises the full 200-unit order at an implied ASP of ~$1–1.5M per aircraft, that represents $200–300M in direct aircraft revenue; operations revenue (per-trip fees) could add an additional $50–100M+ annually once flights scale to 50+ flights per day per hub.

3. Defense/Military Market Opportunity & USAF Archer Contract

Metric: Military eVTOL market size, CAGR, and USAF procurement opportunity

The Military segment constitutes a significant market valued at USD 12,782.63 million in 2024, with its growth projected at a steady CAGR of 9.5% through 2030+. The defense and military application segment contributes around 18% of global eVTOL Aircraft Market demand, with 2,150+ units allocated for reconnaissance, tactical logistics, border patrol, surveillance, search & rescue, and disaster response missions.

USAF Archer Contract: Archer Aviation has secured contracts with the US Air Force (USAF) worth up to $142 million for initial deliveries of the Midnight eVTOL aircraft. The aircraft has a target payload of approximately 1,000 lbs and can accommodate up to four passengers, with low noise profile, making it suitable for personnel transport, logistics support, rescue operations, and reconnaissance. Archer delivered its first Midnight eVTOL for USAF evaluation on July 15, 2024.

USAF Procurement Trajectory: The $142M contract covers initial pilot units (up to 6 aircraft), but the USAF Agility Prime program has evaluated 2,150+ units as a potential allocation for the Department of Defense across multiple use cases. If USAF approves follow-on production orders at even 5–10% of that allocation (100–200 units over 2027–2030), and Archer captures a 30–50% share of DoD eVTOL procurement, that represents 30–100 units of USAF demand, plus additional allied military orders.

Archer Sensitivity: HIGH. Defense revenue is less price-sensitive and has longer contract values than commercial operations. Per A2.3, the USAF partnership represents a credible "first customer" validation that de-risks the commercial business model. However, defense volume is ultimately capped by DoD budgeting cycles and regulatory approval; it is unlikely to exceed 100–150 units annually even at peak, compared to airport shuttle potential of 500+ units/year by 2030.

The procurement volume of eVTOL is projected to grow from 2,157 units in 2031 to 5,280 units by 2035.

Archer Production Roadmap: Archer management plans to ramp up production to 252 aircraft in 2027 and 650 aircraft in 2028. By 2027, the company projects it will be producing 252 aircraft annually at a gross margin of between 40% and 50%, delivering a gross profit of $500 million to $630 million. By 2028, it expects to produce 650 aircraft, with gross profit ballooning to $1.6 billion.

Archer Market Share Projection (by 2030): If industry-wide eVTOL production reaches 5,280 units by 2035 and Archer maintains 252+ units/year through 2027–2030, Archer's implied market share would be 5–10% of global production by 2030, assuming competitive parity with Joby and others. This assumes: (a) Archer successfully achieves FAA Type Certification by 2027–2028; (b) United exercises significant portion of 200-unit order; (c) manufacturing ramp executes as planned.

Risk to Capacity: 2026 is identified as the critical inflection point, marking when the next 18 months become vital for securing certifiable configurations, vertiport sites, and infrastructure partnerships. Any delay in FAA Type Certification (per A2.3, currently expected 2027–2028) would compress Archer's available runway for scaling to 252 units by 2027, forcing reductions in market share assumptions.

Archer Sensitivity: MEDIUM-HIGH. Capacity and supply-side dynamics directly constrain Archer's ability to capture market share. Per A2.3, Type Certification completion is rated MEDIUM sensitivity, affecting TSR by +7% (expected) to ±15% upside/downside. However, the broader sector's capacity constraints (battery supply, vertiport buildout, regulatory approvals) may create supply-side tailwinds for early entrants like Archer.

5. Leisure/Tourism Segment & Adjacent Opportunities

Metric: Leisure eVTOL market growth and scenic flight pricing dynamics

Market Opportunity: Tourism segments are expected to grow 200%+ by 2030 as infrastructure expands. eVTOL sightseeing flights last 15-30 minutes and charge $100-300 per person per flight. EHang 216 flew 15,000+ passengers in 2024-2025, with Guizhou Scenic Tourism Co. ordering 50 EH216-S units for scenic flights.

Archer Entry Point: Archer has not yet targeted the leisure segment explicitly; however, the Midnight aircraft's 4-passenger capacity and low-noise profile make it suitable for scenic tours at premium resort destinations (e.g., Las Vegas, Hawaii, South Florida). Leisure operations require minimal infrastructure (a landing pad, not a full vertiport) and lower regulatory barriers than passenger transport to airports. If Archer dedicated 5–10% of production capacity (25–65 units by 2030) to tour operators, this could yield $50–150M in additional aircraft sales plus recurring operational revenue.

Archer Sensitivity: LOW (0–12 months horizon). Leisure is not a stated priority in A2.3's commercial revenue thesis, which emphasizes USAF and United as primary revenue drivers. However, leisure represents a hedge opportunity: if United or USAF timelines slip, leisure operators could provide near-term revenue diversification and cash flow to sustain R&D and certification spending.
